## Court & Docket Databases

- **[PACER](https://pacer.uscourts.gov/)** — The official U.S. federal judiciary system for accessing bankruptcy, district, and appellate court filings and dockets ($0.10/page, capped at $3.00/document).

- **[PACER Case Locator](https://pcl.uscourts.gov/)** — Free nationwide search index for determining whether a party is involved in federal litigation across all district, bankruptcy, and appellate courts.

- **[CourtListener / RECAP Archive](https://www.courtlistener.com/recap/)** — Free, searchable archive of millions of PACER documents crowdsourced via the RECAP browser extension, maintained by the nonprofit Free Law Project.

- **[PacerMonitor](https://www.pacermonitor.com/)** — Commercial platform for tracking and searching federal district and bankruptcy court cases with enhanced alerting, analytics, and a more intuitive interface than raw PACER.

- **[PacerPro](https://www.pacerpro.com/)** — Workflow automation tool trusted by many AmLaw 100 firms that automatically delivers PACER filings and docket updates to attorneys, reducing manual monitoring.

- **[Bloomberg Law Dockets](https://pro.bloomberglaw.com/)** — Integrated docket search within Bloomberg Law providing court filings alongside legal analytics and secondary sources (subscription required).

- **[Florida-UCLA-LoPucki Bankruptcy Research Database (BRD)](https://lopucki.law.ufl.edu/index.php)** — Comprehensive academic database of all large public company bankruptcy filings since 1979, widely used in bankruptcy research (note: no longer actively maintained as of ~2022, but archival data remains valuable).

- **[Federal Judicial Center Integrated Database (IDB)](https://www.fjc.gov/research/idb)** — Free government dataset of case-level data for all federal civil, criminal, appellate, and bankruptcy cases, useful for large-scale empirical research.

## News & Intelligence Platforms

- **[Octus (formerly Reorg)](https://octus.com/)** — The market-leading credit intelligence platform providing real-time news, legal and financial analysis, and docket tracking across the full credit lifecycle from performing through post-reorg.

- **[Debtwire](https://info.debtwire.com/)** — End-to-end intelligence platform for leveraged capital markets professionals, combining proprietary journalism with predictive analytics (Likely-to-Distress scores) and restructuring case tracking.

- **[9fin](https://www.9fin.com/)** — AI-native debt market intelligence platform offering real-time news, covenant analysis, deal data, and distressed/restructuring coverage across leveraged finance, private credit, and CLOs.

- **[PitchBook LCD (Leveraged Commentary & Data)](https://pitchbook.com/leveraged-commentary-data)** — Market-leading source for leveraged loan and high-yield bond data, news, and indexes, now part of PitchBook/Morningstar, with deep coverage of distressed and default markets.

- **[Bloomberg Terminal](https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/bloomberg-terminal/)** — The industry-standard financial data terminal with extensive fixed-income analytics, credit default swap data, and distressed debt pricing via functions like SRCH, DDIS, and CACS.

- **[Creditflux](https://creditflux.com/)** — Independent news and data service focused on CLOs, leveraged loans, and credit markets, useful for tracking fund flows and market trends in the credit space.

- **[BankruptcyData.com](https://www.bankruptcydata.com/)** — Subscription database from New Generation Research tracking publicly traded and select private company bankruptcy filings since 1986, including the annual Bankruptcy Yearbook & Almanac.

- **[Distressed Debt Investing Blog](https://www.distressed-debt-investing.com/)** — Long-running industry blog (now affiliated with Reorg/Octus) offering commentary and case analysis on distressed situations, founded by Reorg's Kent Collier.

## Data & Analytics Tools

- **[S&P Capital IQ Pro](https://www.spglobal.com/marketintelligence/en/solutions/sp-capital-iq-pro)** — Comprehensive financial data platform with credit analytics, CDS data, recovery rate analysis, and screening tools for identifying stressed and distressed credits.

- **[Moody's CreditView](https://www.moodys.com/web/en/us/capabilities/research-and-data/moodys-creditview.html)** — Moody's analytics platform offering credit ratings, default studies, recovery rate research, and the proprietary Expected Default Frequency (EDF) model for assessing bankruptcy probability.

- **[Covenant Review (acquired by Fitch)](https://www.fitchratings.com/)** — Independent covenant analysis of high-yield bond indentures and loan credit agreements, now part of Fitch Solutions, essential for evaluating liability management exercise risk.

- **[TRACE (FINRA)](https://www.finra.org/finra-data/browse-catalog/trace/bond-trades)** — FINRA's Trade Reporting and Compliance Engine providing real-time transparency into over-the-counter bond transactions, including distressed corporate debt trading activity.

- **[ICE BofA High Yield Indices](https://www.ice.com/fixed-income-data-services/fixed-income-indices)** — Widely used benchmarks for high-yield and distressed debt market performance, including the Distressed Index (tracks bonds trading at 1000+ bps over Treasuries).

- **[Fitch Ratings](https://www.fitchratings.com/)** — Global credit rating agency with detailed issuer research, recovery ratings, and sector-specific default studies relevant to restructuring analysis.

- **[LSEG LPC (Loan Pricing Corporation)](https://www.lseg.com/en/data-analytics/financial-data/lending-data-analytics)** — Leveraged loan market data including primary issuance, secondary trading levels, and loan analytics for tracking distressed loan trading.

## Academic Papers & Research

- **[Altman, E. (1968). "Financial Ratios, Discriminant Analysis and the Prediction of Corporate Bankruptcy." *Journal of Finance*.](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1968.tb00843.x)** — The seminal paper introducing the Z-Score model for bankruptcy prediction, one of the most cited works in all of finance and still widely used by practitioners today.

- **[Altman, E. (2019). "Fifty Years of Z-Scores to Predict the Probability of Corporate Bankruptcy."](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3522672)** — Altman's retrospective on five decades of the Z-Score model, discussing its evolution, applications, and continued relevance in credit analysis.

- **[Altman, E. & Kishore, V. (1996). "Almost Everything You Wanted to Know About Recoveries on Defaulted Bonds." *Financial Analysts Journal*.](https://doi.org/10.2469/faj.v52.n6.2040)** — Foundational study on recovery rates by seniority and industry, essential reading for anyone modeling distressed debt valuations.

- **[Gilson, S.C. (1997). "Transactions Costs and Capital Structure Choice: Evidence from Financially Distressed Firms." *Journal of Finance*.](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1997.tb02740.x)** — Influential paper examining how transaction costs influence whether distressed firms restructure in or out of court, shaping our understanding of Chapter 11 vs. private workouts.

- **[Hotchkiss, E. (1995). "Post-Bankruptcy Performance and Management Turnover." *Journal of Finance*.](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1995.tb04044.x)** — Landmark study finding that many firms emerging from Chapter 11 continue to experience operating difficulties, with implications for post-reorg equity investing strategies.

- **[Baird, D. & Rasmussen, R. (2002). "The End of Bankruptcy." *Stanford Law Review*.](https://doi.org/10.2307/1229682)** — Provocative and widely cited legal scholarship arguing that Chapter 11 has fundamentally changed from a forum for reorganization to a mechanism for going-concern sales.

- **[Ayotte, K. & Morrison, E. (2009). "Creditor Control and Conflict in Chapter 11." *Journal of Legal Analysis*.](https://doi.org/10.1093/jla/1.2.511)** — Empirical analysis of how DIP financing terms give secured creditors de facto control of the reorganization process, with important implications for unsecured creditor recoveries.

- **[Jiang, W., Li, K. & Wang, W. (2012). "Hedge Funds and Chapter 11." *Journal of Finance*.](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2011.01714.x)** — Key empirical study documenting the role of hedge funds in corporate bankruptcies, finding that their involvement generally leads to better outcomes for creditors.

## Books

- **[*Distressed Debt Analysis* — Stephen G. Moyer (2004)](https://jrosspub.com/catalog/business-default-category/finance/distressed-debt-analysis/)** — The definitive practitioner's guide covering bankruptcy law, valuation, capital structure analysis, and investment strategies; widely considered the single most essential book in the field.

- **[*Distress Investing* — Martin J. Whitman & Fernando Diz (2009)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Distress+Investing%3A+Principles+and+Technique-p-9780470117675)** — Practical, case-study-driven guide from the legendary founder of Third Avenue Management covering buy-and-hold distressed credit investing and out-of-court positioning.

- **[*Corporate Financial Distress, Restructuring, and Bankruptcy* — Edward I. Altman & Edith Hotchkiss (4th ed., 2019)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Corporate+Financial+Distress%2C+Restructuring%2C+and+Bankruptcy%3A+Analyze+Leveraged+Finance%2C+Distressed+Debt%2C+and+Bankruptcy%2C+4th+Edition-p-9781119481805)** — The most comprehensive academic-practitioner bridge text, covering prediction models, leveraged finance analysis, and the restructuring process from the creator of the Z-Score.

- **[*The Credit Investor's Handbook* — Michael Gatto (2023)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Credit+Investor%27s+Handbook-p-9781394196043)** — Modern guide covering leveraged loans, high-yield bonds, and distressed debt with extensive coverage of recent aggressive issuer maneuvers and liability management exercises.

- **[*Creating Value Through Corporate Restructuring* — Stuart C. Gilson (2010)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Creating+Value+Through+Corporate+Restructuring%3A+Case+Studies+in+Bankruptcies%2C+Buyouts%2C+and+Breakups%2C+2nd+Edition-p-9780470503522)** — Harvard Business School case studies covering bankruptcies, buyouts, and breakups, bridging academic rigor with real-world restructuring scenarios.

- **[*The Art of Distressed M&A* — H. Peter Nesvold, Jeffrey Anapolsky & Alexandra Lajoux (2011)](https://www.mhprofessional.com/the-art-of-distressed-m-a-9780071750196-usa)** — Comprehensive guide to buying, selling, and financing troubled companies, covering 363 sales, stalking horse bids, and the intersection of M&A and bankruptcy.

- **[*A Pragmatist's Guide to Leveraged Finance* — Robert S. Kricheff (2012)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/A+Pragmatist%27s+Guide+to+Leveraged+Finance-p-9780133552768)** — Accessible guide to credit analysis for below-investment-grade bonds and loans, providing the foundational leveraged finance knowledge needed before tackling distressed situations.

- **[*The Vulture Investors* — Hilary Rosenberg (2000)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Vulture+Investors%2C+Revised+and+Updated-p-9780471361893)** — Narrative-driven account of distressed debt investors from the 1980s and 1990s, offering unparalleled insight into the psychology and competitive dynamics of vulture investing.

- **[*The Most Important Thing* — Howard Marks (2011)](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/the-most-important-thing-howard-marks)** — Investment philosophy from the Oaktree Capital co-founder covering risk, market cycles, and contrarian thinking, essential context for understanding the distressed investing mindset.

## Regulatory & Legal Resources

- **[Title 11, U.S. Code (Bankruptcy Code) — Cornell LII](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11)** — The full text of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code with annotations and cross-references, freely accessible through Cornell Law's Legal Information Institute.

- **[Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure (FRBP)](https://www.uscourts.gov/forms-rules/current-rules-practice-procedure/federal-rules-bankruptcy-procedure)** — Official procedural rules governing bankruptcy case practice, including the official forms, maintained by the Administrative Office of the U.S. Courts.

- **[Bankruptcy Basics — U.S. Courts](https://www.uscourts.gov/court-programs/bankruptcy/bankruptcy-basics)** — Plain-language overview of Chapters 7, 11, 12, and 13 from the federal judiciary, useful as a quick reference for the mechanics of each type of bankruptcy proceeding.

- **[Local Rules — U.S. Bankruptcy Court, District of Delaware](https://www.deb.uscourts.gov/local-rules)** — Local rules for the District of Delaware, one of the two most common venues for large corporate Chapter 11 filings, essential reading for practitioners in mega-cases.

- **[Local Rules — U.S. Bankruptcy Court, Southern District of New York](https://www.nysb.uscourts.gov/local-rules)** — Local rules for SDNY, the other dominant venue for major corporate restructurings, including the complex case procedures used in large Chapter 11 cases.

- **[Collier on Bankruptcy (LexisNexis)](https://www.lexisnexis.com/supp/lawschool/collier-on-bankruptcy.aspx)** — The leading multi-volume treatise on bankruptcy law, the authoritative secondary source cited by courts and practitioners for interpreting the Bankruptcy Code (subscription required).

- **[ABI Law Review](https://www.abi.org/abi-journal)** — Scholarly and practitioner journal published by the American Bankruptcy Institute, featuring in-depth articles on current issues in bankruptcy law and practice.

- **[SEC EDGAR Full-Text Search](https://efts.sec.gov/LATEST/search-index?q=)** — SEC's filing database for searching 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, and other corporate filings, critical for analyzing the financial condition of companies approaching or in distress.

## Key GitHub Repos & Open Datasets

- **[Free Law Project / CourtListener](https://github.com/freelawproject/courtlistener)** — Open-source platform behind CourtListener and the RECAP Archive, with APIs for accessing millions of federal court dockets and documents including bankruptcy cases.

- **[RECAP Extensions](https://github.com/freelawproject/recap)** — Open-source browser extensions (Chrome, Firefox, Safari) that crowdsource PACER documents into the free RECAP Archive, reducing the cost barrier to court record access.

- **[henriarnoUG/ECL](https://github.com/henriarnoUG/ECL)** — Academic dataset combining SEC 10-K filings (text + financials) with LoPucki BRD bankruptcy labels for training and benchmarking bankruptcy prediction models.

- **[Federal Judicial Center Integrated Database](https://www.fjc.gov/research/idb)** — Free bulk data on all federal court cases (civil, criminal, bankruptcy, appellate), downloadable in flat file format for empirical legal and financial research.

- **[SEC EDGAR Full-Text Search System](https://efts.sec.gov/LATEST/search-index?q=)** — Free API and search interface for accessing corporate filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K), essential for analyzing distressed companies' financial disclosures.

- **[hodchuy/Bankruptcy-prediction](https://github.com/hodchuy/Bankruptcy-prediction)** — Machine learning project implementing SVM, Decision Tree, KNN, and Random Forest models for predicting U.S. service company bankruptcies using financial ratios.

- **[CourtListener Bulk Data](https://www.courtlistener.com/api/bulk-info/)** — Bulk downloads of millions of federal court dockets, opinions, and PACER data in CSV and JSON format, enabling large-scale bankruptcy research.
